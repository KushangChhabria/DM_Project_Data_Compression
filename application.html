Problems Insight



Modern computers often generate files of very large sizes. For example, audio files often run to megabytes, while high definition video can be gigabytes in size. Such files require lots of storage space, and because of their size, they are difficult to transmit. These problems can be overcome by using compression.

Prior to the development of data compression techniques people faced several challenges:


  • Limited Storage Capacity: Without compression, data took up more space, resulting in the need for larger and more expensive storage solutions.

  • Slow Data Transmission: Larger file sizes led to slower transmission speeds, causing delays and inefficiencies.

  • Limited Portability: Larger files are diificult to transport via CDs or floppy discs, and thus take longer time to transfer data.

  • Processing Time: It is more difficult to perform functions such as sorting, searching and analyzing large data-files. This caused lesser productivity.

  • Backup Storage: Backing up large amount of data required significant amount of resources and time.

  • Accessibility: Uncompressed large datasets were harder to share and distribute, especially in bounded network bandwidth.
